Episode 2: Simplicity as Empowerment

Published on May 9, 2026

Introduction

In this episode, we invite British-American designer Jony Ive to talk about how minimalist design can empower people and give them inspiration.

For Jony Ive, a common misunderstanding is that simplicity means just removing things. When this happens, the result is often a product that looks clean, but feels empty or without life. Many minimalist or modernist designs fall into this trap.

Instead, Ive believes the goal of design is to bring order to chaos. It is about clearly expressing what something is, what it is for, and how it fits into people’s lives. In his view, good design is not about reducing meaning, but about making meaning clearer. In the technology world, especially in Silicon Valley, humor and joy are often missing. Products are usually complex, and joy is sometimes seen as unimportant or even childish. But Ive believes this is a mistake. He thinks the mindset of the creator always shows in the work. If the designer feels stressed or anxious, the product will also feel like that. For him, simplicity is not only a visual result, but also a way of thinking and working. It comes from care, focus, and emotional awareness in the design process. As he often suggests, design is not just about how things look, but about how they make people feel and behave.¹

Through this podcast, we explore how Jony Ive and Apple approach design: a process that moves from complexity, through reduction, and finally toward clarity and emotional connection.

Footnote

¹ “Jony Ive 谈设计的挑战.” Jianglin Design. Accessed April 19, 2026.
