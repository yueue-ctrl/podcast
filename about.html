Episodes Hosts About

We throw the word minimalism around constantly. We use it to sell furniture, justify interfaces, praise architecture, and declutter our homes. But somewhere between Mies van der Rohe's radical manifesto and a beige MUJI shelf, the idea lost its edges.

FORM is a podcast about recovering those edges.

Hosted by two practicing designers, each episode uses minimalism as a lens to examine the deeper questions that drive design theory — questions designers encounter every day but rarely stop to articulate. What does it actually mean for form to follow function? When does an aesthetic judgment quietly become a moral one? Is design ever truly neutral, or does every visual decision carry an ideology?

We move through history not as a timeline, but as an argument. From the Bauhaus workshops to Dieter Rams' ten commandments, from Apple's obsessive reduction to the global spread of Scandinavian flatness — each moment is a case study in how ideas about less shaped the way the world looks, and who that world was designed for.

FORM is not a how-to show. There are no tutorials, no trend reports, no advice on becoming a better minimalist. It is a show about thinking — about learning to see the theory embedded in every design decision, and asking whether we still believe it.

Because the most important question minimalism ever raised was never how little can we use?
